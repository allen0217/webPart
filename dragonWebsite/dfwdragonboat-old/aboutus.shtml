About Marco Polo World Foundation (MPWF)

Marco Polo World Foundation is a non-profit 501(c)(3) organization founded in 2007. We are a non-religious and non-partisan organization with a sole focus on promoting intercultural understanding. Our mission is to introduce to our community the sport of dragon boat racing, and to showcase Asian culture, thus to create better understanding and acceptance of diversity in the metroplex community. We launched the Annual DFW Dragon Boat, Kite and Lantern Festival as an effort to pursue our mission, and to celebrate Asian-Pacific American Heritage Month. Through this festival, we hope to bring together organizations from all different sectors of the community, and encourage local, regional and international interaction..

This year, Marco Polo World Foundation is expanding our mission by offering scholarship awards to high school and college students who show dedication to promoting inter-cultural understanding. For more information about the scholarship please visit http://dfwdragonboatfestival.com/scholarship.shtml . Application deadline is May 8th, 2012

Marco Polo World Foundation provides the following programs:
1. Annual DFW Dragon boat, Kite and Lantern Festival
2. College scholarship for high school graduates and college students.
Marco Polo World Foundation will provide more programs and events to fulfill its mission. Please visit http://dfwdragonboatfestival.com regularly for updates.

Diana Meilan Wang

Co-founder, Chairperson and President of the Marco Polo World Foundation.

Meilan has devoted herself in organizing and implementing the Annual DFW Dragon Boat, Kite, and Lantern Festival since 2007. Meilan is an active volunteer in the community, President of Organization of Chinese Americans (OCA), President of Irving International Women's Consortium (IIWC), lifetime and Board member of the DFW Asian American Citizens Council (DFWAACC), and a member of the Greater Irving - Las Colinas Chamber of Commerce as well as the Dallas Regional Chamber. Meilan is the owner of Sen Can International and Pageant Director of Miss Asian American Texas 2009-2013. Before coming to the US, Meilan worked at Xiamen TV station in one of China's booming economic zones, as a Producer/Director of Entertainment programs.

Eugene Shen

Secretary

Mr. Eugene Shen serves as the Senior Coordinator over International Business Development for the Office of Economic Development of the City of Dallas. He is also the Asia Liaison for the City of Dallas. His responsibilities include international business recruitment, expansion and retention, marketing and information services. Before joining the City of Dallas Economic Development team, Mr. Shen served as the president of Prime Asian Journal, overseeing the whole operation of the Dallas based publishing company. Mr. Shen received his B.A. in Political Science from Soochow University of Taiwan, and his graduate degrees in International Political Economy from the University of Maryland.

Larry Jeffus

Board member

Larry was elected to the Garland City Council in 2006 and is in his third term. In November of 2003 he was appointed by Governor Rick Perry to the Texas Workforce Investment Council (TWIC). The Council is made up of seven agency heads and representatives from business and industry, labor, community-based organizations, and education. TWIC is tasked with providing recommendations to the governor’s office and coordinating the efforts of the state agencies to develop an educated and skilled workforce. From 2005 to 2009 he represented TWIC on the advisory board for the Texas Technology Access Program (TTAP). TTAP is a statewide program which helps individuals with disabilities identify the most appropriate adaptive devices to help them in their daily lives and/or the world of work. From 2009 to the present he now serves on the Apprenticeship Project Leadership Team, which is helping to implement apprenticeship programs for skilled job training. Larry has been heavily involved in economic development in Garland and North Texas. He has served for four years on the Garland Economic Development Steering Committee, and he actively recruits new businesses for Garland. Larry is a consultant and author who retired after 40 years of teaching. As an author, Larry has published more than 200 technical textbooks since 1976.

About Marco Polo World Foundation (MPWF)

Our mission is to promote intercultural understanding by sharing and celebrating International Culture with all people of the region and across America.

The Marco Polo World Foundation (MPWF) is a public charity, organized as a non-profit 501(c) (3), non-religious, non-partisan organization founded in 2007. MPWF provides four programs:


1. Outreach - Annual DFW Dragon Boat, Kite and Lantern Festival

                 We launched the Annual DFW Dragon Boat, Kite and Lantern Festival as an effort to pursue our mission, as well as to showcase international culture and to celebrate Asian-Pacific American Heritage Month

2. Scholarships. Learn more about the scholarship program.

               The MPWF scholarship is awarded to students who show dedication to promoting intercultural understanding and serving the community through volunteerism.

3. Internships. Learn more about the Internship program.

                The internship can have a great opportunity to explore his/her self and the world. Improve their organizational and interaction skills, expose to different job opportunities and expanded network of associates and professionals

4. Asian Culture Seminar. Learn more about Asian Culture Seminar.

                   The MPWF brings introductory culture courses to school districts in the DFW metroplex.


Through these programs, we bring together individuals and organizations from different sectors of the community by encouraging local, regional and international groups to engage with a variety of social and civic betterment efforts.

For more information about our programs, please visit us on Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, or our website at www.marcopoloworldfoundation.org
We encourage individuals and organizations to donate funds to the foundation, that we can continue our current programs and develop new programs for the community.

Diana Wang Madsen

Founder, Chairwoman of the board and President

Diana Wang Madsen is founder, Chairperson of the board & President of the Marco Polo World Foundation. In 2007, she started the first Annual DFW Dragon Boat, Kite, and Lantern Festival in North Texas. Since then, she has devoted herself to organizing this event every year and its continuous development. Diana is an active volunteer for the community and has served several different organizations. She was elected for fourth term as the President of the DFW Chapter of the Organization of Chinese Americans (OCA) in 2012, 2013, 2015 and 2016. She is the Former President of the Irving International Women's Consortium (IIWC), and Former Vice-President of the Asian American Cultural Association (AACA). She is a lifetime member and board member of the DFW Asian American Citizens Council (DFWAACC), and a member of the Greater Irving - Las Colinas Chamber of Commerce, as well as the Dallas Regional Chamber. She also serving on the Project Advisory Committee of Richland College Asian American and Native American Pacific Islander Serving Institution (AANAPISI) and as director of the Miss Asian American Texas Pageant from 2009 to 2015. As an entrepreneur, Diana is the owner of Sen Can International LLC. Before coming to the US, Diana worked as a producer/director of sports and entertainment programs at Xiamen TV station in one of China's booming economic zones. For her dedication to community service and enrichment of Asian Cultural Heritage, Diana has been awarded Community Service Excellence Award (2009, 2010, 2011), Best In Community Outreach Award (2012), Contribution to Asian Culture Award (2014) from different organizations across North Texas.
